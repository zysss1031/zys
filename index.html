01 — Project

EdTech Feedback Product Assessment

AI Evaluation / Research Design / Product Insight
RoleLed a 4-person workstream within a 10-person consulting practicum team.
The Problem

AI-generated writing feedback can appear polished, but that does not necessarily mean it is useful. The challenge was to determine how to evaluate whether feedback actually supports teachers and students in meaningful ways.

My Approach
01
Clarify the question
Define what effective AI feedback should mean and identify what the product should be judged against.
→
02
Gather evidence
Use literature review and educator interviews to identify recurring needs and expectations.
→
03
Build a framework
Translate qualitative needs into structured evaluation dimensions and clearer judgment criteria.
→
04
Apply the framework
Use 120 synthetic essays and a dual-model LLM-as-judge process to compare performance.
→
05
Generate insight
Identify grammar coverage gaps and support targeted improvement recommendations.
Key Outputs
  • Literature synthesis
  • Interview-based user insight
  • Structured evaluation framework
  • Client-facing white paper
  • Product improvement recommendations
Result / Insight

The project turned an ambiguous product question into a clearer evaluative framework. The work created a more grounded way to judge whether feedback was educationally useful.

What I Learned

This project reinforced the importance of defining the right criteria before evaluating outputs. It also strengthened my interest in work that sits between human judgment and technical systems.

02 — Project

Dropbox Business Performance Assessment

Financial Analysis / Modeling / Business Interpretation
RoleWorked in a 3-person team on financial reconstruction, ratio analysis, and final presentation development.
The Problem

Public filings contain a large amount of useful information, but that information is not automatically structured for analysis. The challenge was to reconstruct Dropbox's financial picture in a way that was accurate, auditable, and easy to interpret.

My Approach
01
Define the goal
Clarify the business questions and determine which performance areas matter most.
→
02
Rebuild the structure
Reconstruct historical statements from filings and ensure each metric can be traced to source.
→
03
Diagnose performance
Examine profitability trends, efficiency, and capital structure considerations.
→
04
Translate to story
Convert workbook outputs into presentation logic and summarize the key business implications.
→
05
Final result
Create an auditable workbook and deliver a clearer, presentation-ready business view.
Key Outputs
  • Auditable Excel workbook
  • Historical financial reconstruction
  • Ratio and trend analysis
  • Next-year income forecast
  • Final presentation deck
Result / Insight

This project strengthened my ability to move from raw source material to structured interpretation. Rigorous analysis becomes more useful when it is traceable, legible, and clearly communicated.

What I Learned

Good business analysis is not only about calculation. It also depends on building a system where evidence, logic, and communication stay aligned.

03 — Project

Mars Capital Portfolio Reporting Support

Portfolio Reporting / Reconciliation / Performance Review
RolePortfolio Management Intern
The Problem

Portfolio reporting depends on consistency across holdings, transactions, cash flows, and valuation changes. The challenge was to bring fragmented reporting inputs into a cleaner structure so that performance could be reviewed more reliably.

My Approach
01
Review inputs
Gather beginning and ending balances and identify relevant holdings, transactions, and cash flow records.
→
02
Reconcile & standardize
Align portfolio changes across the reporting period and standardize the reporting structure.
→
03
Build reporting views
Create clearer templates and organize allocation and performance changes into readable summaries.
→
04
Support review
Highlight YTD public and private performance and summarize transaction activity for review.
→
05
Final result
Produce more structured reporting outputs and improve month-end review readability.
Key Outputs
  • Reconciled reporting views
  • Standardized portfolio reporting template
  • Allocation and performance summaries
  • Transaction and cash flow rollups
Result / Insight

This work improved the clarity and consistency of portfolio reporting. Useful analysis often depends on careful reconciliation before interpretation begins.

What I Learned

Strong reporting depends on precision in the underlying structure. Clear outputs usually come from disciplined organization upstream.
